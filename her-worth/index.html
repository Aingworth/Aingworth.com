The recognition economy is not gender-neutral. Women-led businesses and women executives face a compounding problem: the signals that AI systems use to determine authority have historically been easier to build for those who were already in the room. Thought leadership platforms, media coverage, speaking stages, and citation networks all carry structural bias that pre-dates the AI era.

That bias does not disappear when the search engine becomes an AI. In some ways it amplifies. AI models learn from existing authority signals. If those signals underrepresent women's expertise, the model underrepresents women's expertise in its answers.

HER-Worth exists because the solution to a structural problem is a structural one. Not a mindset fix. Not a confidence workshop. Not a list of social media tips. A real, systematic approach to building the kind of authority that AI systems can find, interpret, and cite.

The same framework. Applied with full awareness of the additional context that women in business are navigating every single day.

The Framework

The Recognition Triangle, applied to your reality.

The Recognition Triangle maps brand authority across three dimensions. All three need to work together for AI systems to recognise and cite your expertise consistently. HER-Worth applies the framework with full awareness of the structural challenges women face in building each dimension.

Clarity

How clearly your brand communicates what you stand for and who you serve. Many women hold this internally but have not yet made it legible to external systems. We articulate it, then structure it.

Credibility

The external signals that verify your expertise: publications, citations, media, speaking, and platform presence. These have historically been harder for women to accumulate. We build them deliberately.

Earned Authority

The compounding recognition that comes from consistent, genuine expertise signals over time. Not manufactured. Not bought. Built, so it holds up when AI systems evaluate it.
